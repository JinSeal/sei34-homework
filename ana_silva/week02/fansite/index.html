Fran Allen

Frances "Fran" Elizabeth Allen is an American computer scientist known for her contributions to programming language compilers.

Frances is an IBM Fellow Emerita at IBM’s T. J. Watson Research Center, NY. Starting as a programmer at IBM Research in July 1957, her first assignment was to teach the research scientists Fortran, a new high-level language IBM had announced 3 months earlier. This was the beginning of Allen’s career-long focus on compilers for high-performance computing.

PUT THIS IN AN INSET A compiler is a special program that processes statements written in a particular programming language and turns them into machine language or "code" that a computer's processor uses.

Where was Frances born? She was born in Plattsburg, New York, but grew up in a farm in Peru, New York.

Does Frances have any siblings? Yes, she is the oldest of six children.

What did her parents do? Her father was a farmer, and her mother an elementary-school teacher.
