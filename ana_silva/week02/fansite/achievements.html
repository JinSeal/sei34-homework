Fran Allen's Achievements

Awards

  • 2010, elected to the National Academy of Sciences
  • 2009, honorary doctor of science degree from McGill University
  • 2006 Turing Award for her work in high-performance computing (first woman to receive it), equivalent of the Nobel Prize for computing and is given by the Association for Computing Machinery.
  • 2004, Computer Pioneer Award of the IEEE Computer Society
  • 2004, ABIE Award for Technical Leadership from the Anita Borg Institute
  • 2002 Augusta Ada Lovelace Award from the Association for Women in Computing
  • 2001, elected to the American Philosophical Society
  • 2000, Fellow of the Computer History Museum
  • 1997, IEEE Computer Society Charles Babbage Award
  • 1997, inducted into the WITI Hall of Fame
  • 1994, Fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)
  • 1994, nominated a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences
  • 1991, Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)
  • 1987, elected to the National Academy of Engineering

In high school Fran was greatly inspired by her math teacher, and set out to become a math teacher herself.

She made up a course called "General Maths" to help the kids that were going to be farmers and could not afford to go to college. It helped them with practical things like keeping books, doing taxes, measurements, etc.

Fran enrolled at the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor and earned an MA in mathematics.
