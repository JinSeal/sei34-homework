Biography of Sophie Wilson

Sophie Mary Wilson, born 1957 in Yorkshire England.

Studied computer science at University of Cambridge. In a university Easter break Wilson designed a microcomputer which was used to electronically control feed to cows. This was a hint of things to come.

In July 1981, Wilson extended a BASIC programming language to help win a project to put personal computers into the British education system. Her BBC BASIC was developed by Wilson for the next 15 years.

In October 1983, Wilson began designing the instructions for one of the first Reduced Instrucction Set Computer (RISC) processors, the Acorn RISC Machine (ARM). The ARM1 was released in 1985. This processor type is now a licencsed CPU core that by 2012 was being used in 95% of smartphones.

Wilson was listed in 2001 as number 8 in a top 15 women in Tech history article.

Wilson was appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) in the 2019 Birthday Honours for services to computing.

You can still find Sophie living in Lode, Cambridgeshire.
